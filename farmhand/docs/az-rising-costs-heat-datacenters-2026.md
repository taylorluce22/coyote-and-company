Sourced knowledge base for the rising-cost / solar-as-hedge thesis (see

**[fact] / [projection] / [contested] / [industry-claim]** with source +

## 1. Rising electricity costs

- **[fact]** APS filed its current rate case **June 2025**, seeking a
  **13.99% net revenue increase** — APS says ~**$20/month more** for a
  typical residential customer at **1,000 kWh**; new rates targeted for
  the **second half of 2026** if approved.
  Source: APS Newsroom, 2025-06-13
  (aps.com/en/About/Our-Company/Newsroom/Articles/APS_Requests_Rate_Adjustment…);
  AZFamily, 2025-06-17.
- **[fact]** This is on top of APS's **~8% increase effective March 8,
  2024** (~$10.50/mo for a 1,050 kWh home, ACC Decision 79293). The asks
  keep coming — two increases in the current cycle.
  Source: APS Newsroom 2024-02-23; 12News (Joe Dana) 2024-03-07; ABC15
  (Anne Ryman) 2025-09-17.
- **[fact]** APS's 2026 case also proposes **>45% increase for extra-large
  users like data centers**, which APS frames as customer protection.
  Source: APS rate-case page; AZFamily 2026-05-17.
- **[fact]** SRP changed pricing effective the **Nov 2025 billing cycle** —
  an **overall 2.4% price increase** ($169M base-revenue increase, partly
  offset by a $68.7M fuel decrease).
  Source: SRP overview-residential-changes page; SRP Media 2025-10-30.
- **[fact]** Arizona's average **residential bill was $160.24/mo in 2024**
  (1,075 kWh at 14.91¢/kWh).
  Source: EIA Table 5A (2024, released 2025-10); EIA AZ state profile.
- **[projection]** EIA historical data: AZ average residential bill rose
  from **~$120.51 (2014) → $160.24 (2024)** = **+33% over 10 years**
  (~2.89% CAGR → doubling in ~24 years at that pace). On the **recent
  5-year** trend (**~$126.09 in 2019 → $160.24 in 2024**, ~4.91% CAGR),
  the doubling horizon is **~14.5 years**.
  Source: EIA historical state workbook (HS861 2010–), released 2025-10-16.
- **[contested]** "Phoenix bills will double soon" as a blanket claim is
  **too strong**. Honest version: bills have clearly trended up, APS
  already did ~8% (2024) and wants ~14% (2026), and the recent pace is
  faster than the 2010s — but the exact doubling horizon depends on
  utility, plan, and household load. **Content rule: give BOTH horizons
  (~15 yr recent pace / ~24 yr decade pace) and never a fixed schedule.**

## 2. Extreme heat & rising neighborhood temperatures

- **[fact]** **2024 was Phoenix's warmest year on record; 2025 second
  warmest.** Longest 100°F+ streak is now **113 days** (May 27–Sep 16,
  2024). July 2023 was the first month in Phoenix history to average above
  100°F.
  Source: NWS Phoenix year-in-review 2023/2024/2025; NWS Extreme Temps.
- **[fact]** Overnight lows increasingly don't cool off — record counts of
  nights never dropping below 90°F; a June 2024 warm-low of 95°F. This
  drives nighttime A/C demand (and evening-peak bills).
  Source: AZFamily 2024-06-27, 2024-08-14.
- **[fact]** Maricopa County heat-related deaths: **645 (2023) → 608
  (2024) → 430 (2025)** — a serious, if recently declining, burden.
  Source: Maricopa County Public Health 2025-06-04 & 2026-04-01 report;
  ABC15 2025-09-29.
- **[fact]** ASU study (May 2026): **data-center waste heat can raise air
  temps in downwind Phoenix neighborhoods by 1–4°F.**
  Source: ASU News 2026-05-18; AZFamily (Steven Sarabia) 2026-05-19.
- **[fact]** Heat = system stress: SRP set a **new 8,542 MW system peak**
  in an Aug 2025 heat event; APS customers reported bill spikes in extreme
  heat.
  Source: SRP Media 2025-08-08; ABC15 (Jorge Torres) 2024-08-12.

## 3. Data centers, load growth & customer impact

- **[fact]** APS: data centers were **~5% of its peak demand in 2025**;
  Phoenix metro ranks **#2 in North America** for proposed data-center
  development; an average data center needs as much energy as **~64,000
  homes**, running near peak **24/7**.
  Source: APS data-centers page.
- **[fact]** SRP: large data centers were **5.1% of summer-2025 peak** and
  are its **fastest-growing customer segment**; some individual centers
  carry **200 MW** load.
  Source: SRP data-center-facts page; SRP Media 2025-08-08.
- **[fact]** APS long-range planning: **>13,000 MW needed by 2031, >14,820
  MW by 2038**, peak demand growing **~40% by 2031**.
  Source: APS Newsroom 2023-11-01; ACC IRP workshop 2024-08-01.
- **[contested]** **Do data centers raise home bills?** Live dispute. APS
  and SRP say rate design makes large users pay their own way (APS's +45%
  data-center proposal, SRP's C&I infrastructure cost rules); AG Kris Mayes
  and consumer advocates challenge whether cost-shifting is fully avoided.
  **Content rule: present as a dispute, never as settled.**
  Source: APS & SRP pages; AZFamily 2026-03-03 (Mayes).
- **[contested]** Data-center **water** use is mixed/incomplete evidence
  (Grist: industrial water ~6% of Mesa's potable use in 2024); the
  **power-system** story is the better-supported homeowner angle.
  Source: Grist 2026-03-04; AZFamily 2026-02-25.

## 4. Grid reliability — the honest boundary

- **[fact]** NERC 2025 Summer Reliability Assessment (WECC-SW): reserve
  margins **not anticipated to fall below** reference — **29% existing-
  certain vs. 23% reference**. The bulk grid is **NOT** officially
  described as presently failing.
  Source: NERC 2025 SRA, 2025-05-12.
- **[projection]** Honest takeaway: utilities are **planning for very large
  load growth** and procuring flexible capacity; evening peaks and future
  capacity are increasingly valuable. This supports "tightening economics
  and planning pressure," **not** "grid collapse." **Content rule: never
  say the grid is unstable/failing. Say utilities are paying up for evening
  flexibility because peaks and growth are straining the plan.**

## 5. Rate design — SRP (FY26 ratebook, published 2025-02-28)

- **[fact]** Legacy export plan **E-13** (TOU Export) runs through the
  **Nov 2029 billing cycle**, then remaining customers move to **E-28**.
  Current plans are **grandfathered only until Nov 2029**.
  Source: SRP FY26 ratebook; SRP Media 2025-10-30.
- **[fact]** The "3–8pm → 5–10pm" shift is **plan-specific, not
  universal**: new **E-16 "Manage Demand 5–10pm"** = on-peak **5–10pm
  year-round**; new **E-28** = on-peak **6–9pm year-round**; older legacy
  plans used 3–6pm / 4–7pm.
  Source: SRP FY26 ratebook.
- **[fact]** **E-28** summer energy: 18.91¢ on-peak / 15.12¢ off-peak /
  4.01¢ super-off-peak; **July–Aug "summer peak" on-peak = 40.26¢/kWh.**
  Super-off-peak window 8am–3pm daily.
  Source: SRP FY26 ratebook.
- **[fact]** **E-16** (demand) summer energy: 12.57¢ on-peak / 9.95¢
  off-peak / 3.93¢ super-off-peak (July–Aug on-peak 16.54¢), **plus**
  demand charges averaging **$13.64/kW summer, $17.86/kW Jul–Aug, $9.69/kW
  winter**.
  Source: SRP FY26 ratebook.
- **[fact]** **SRP solar export credit = 3.45¢/kWh** (E-13/E-16/E-28),
  recalculated each **May** starting May 2026.
  Source: SRP FY26 ratebook.

## 6. Rate design — APS (posted tariffs, effective 2024-03-08)

- **[fact]** APS **TOU-E** on-peak **4–7pm weekday year-round**; winter
  super-off-peak 10am–3pm (Nov–Apr). Energy: **34.396¢ summer on-peak /
  12.345¢ summer off-peak**; 32.543¢ / 12.351¢ winter; 3.495¢ winter
  super-off-peak.
  Source: APS R-TOU-E tariff (Decision 79293).
- **[fact]** APS **R-3** demand plan: same 4–7pm on-peak; demand charge
  **$19.585/kW summer, $13.747/kW winter**; on-peak energy 14.227¢ /
  off-peak 5.943¢ summer.
  Source: APS R-3 tariff.
- **[fact]** APS solar export = **Resource Comparison Proxy (RCP)**,
  currently **6.17¢/kWh** (effective 2025-09-01). RCP resets each Sept,
  **can't drop >10%/yr**, locked **10 years** from interconnection — i.e.,
  a **declining** export rate for each new cohort.
  Source: APS RCP plan of administration (Decision 79482); APS 2026
  interconnection forum deck.
- **[fact]** Until the pending case is decided, **APS residential rates
  remain the March 8, 2024 tariffs.**
  Source: APS rate-case page.

## 7. VPP / home-battery programs — why they matter

- **[fact]** **APS Storage Rewards Pilot**: 5-year pilot, up to 5,000
  participants, pays **$110 per average kW for the season** — APS runs
  **one season** (summer, **May 1–Oct 31**), up to **60 events**, 4–10pm,
  1–4 hours each, bill-credited after Oct 31. → **~$110/avg kW per year.**
  Source: APS Storage Rewards / chargingrewards.com/apsstoragerewards;
  ACC 2025-03-13.
- **[fact]** **SRP Battery Partner**: 5-year pilot ending Apr 30, 2030,
  up to 5,000 customers, pays **$55 per average kW per season, twice a
  year** (summer + winter), bill-credited after each season; never
  discharges below 20%; app notifications + opt-outs. Program terms:
  "$110/kW per year divided into 2 seasonal payments." → **~$110/avg kW
  per year.**
  Source: SRP Battery Partner page; SRP program T&Cs (2025-11-06).
- **[fact]** **Annual pay is roughly the SAME (~$110/avg kW/yr)** — APS
  pays it in one summer season; SRP splits it across summer + winter. **Do
  NOT say "APS pays more."** Verified 2026-07-21 via both program pages.
- **[fact]** Both utilities explicitly tie these to **grid reliability**
  and integrating batteries during high-load hours.
  Source: APS & SRP program pages.
- **[projection]** Honest framing: these programs prove **dispatchable home
  battery power has real economic + operational value in AZ's evening/high-
  load hours** — and utilities will pay homeowners for it. They do **not**
  prove present instability (see §4). **Content angle: "look how much
  they'll pay for access to your evening battery" — not "the grid is
  broken."**

## 8. Solar + battery viability in Arizona

- **[fact]** EnergySage AZ: average payback **~12.94 years**; average
  **~$37,497 saved over 25 years** after upfront cost (average system).
  Source: EnergySage AZ pages (crawled 2026).
- **[projection]** The tariff spread is the clearest support for the
  thesis: with SRP export at **3.45¢** and evening imports in the teens to
  **40¢+**, and APS's steep TOU-E spread, **self-consuming your own solar
  in the evening (via battery) is often worth more than exporting midday**.
  Strongest in SRP territory, still meaningful for APS.
  Source: APS TOU-E tariff; SRP FY26 ratebook.
- **[fact]** SRP's own materials say batteries are most useful **after the
  sun goes down**, ideal for TOU/demand plans, and enable peak shaving.
  Source: SRP solar-battery-storage page.
- **[fact]** Resilience: local extreme-heat outages (e.g., ~1,000+ Goodyear
  homes, July 2025; hundreds out in Aug 2025 storms) make battery backup a
  real safety story in a 113-day-of-100°F summer.
  Source: ABC15 2025-07-09, 2025-08-14.
- **[contested]** "Solar + battery is a slam-dunk for everyone" is too
  broad. Strongest for high evening use, TOU/demand exposure, outage
  sensitivity, good roof access; weakest for low-usage/shaded/hard-to-
  finance homes. Any individualized ROI without a usage profile is
  **unverified**.

## 10. What's solid vs. what's shaky (the honest guardrails)

- **Solid:** AZ bills rising over time (EIA); APS ~8% (2024) + ~14% ask
  (2026); SRP redesign + Nov-2029 grandfathering; low export credits vs.
  high evening imports; record heat + heat-death burden; active utility
  battery programs; large planned load growth. **Lead with these.**
- **Support-level (projection):** "owning + shifting more of your own solar
  into the evening is a better hedge than it used to be" — defensible.
  Better than "everyone must buy solar+battery now."
- **Shaky — handle with the rules above:** (a) "grid is unstable" — NO;
  say utilities are paying up for evening flexibility. (b) "bills will
  double on a schedule" — give both horizons, no fixed date. (c) "data
  centers directly raise your bill" — present as a live dispute. (d) water
  claims — power is the better-supported story.
