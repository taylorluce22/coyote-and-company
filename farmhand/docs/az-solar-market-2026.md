# How Solar Is Sold in Metro Phoenix (2026) — Knowledge Base

Distilled July 2026 from a deep-research pass (Wood Mackenzie, EnergySage,
AZ AG enforcement records, BBB, survey data). Fact-check source of truth —
labels: **[fact]**, **[projection]**, **[survey]**, **[interested-party]**,
**[weak-evidence]**. Numbers current as of mid-July 2026.

## The post-credit financing landscape

- [fact] The §25D residential purchase credit expired Dec 31, 2025 under
  OBBBA — no phase-out. Homeowner purchases installed after that date get
  $0 federal.
- [fact] Commercial entities keep the §48E credit through 2027–28, which
  is WHY the market shifted to third-party ownership: leases/PPAs let the
  finance company claim a credit the homeowner can't. This is a tax-code
  answer, not a sales-tactic answer — explaining it honestly reframes
  "why is everyone pushing leases now."
- [fact→projection] TPO share of new residential installs: 45% in 2024
  (highest since 2016), forecast ~69–70% in 2026 (Wood Mackenzie/Aurora).
- [fact] "Prepaid TPO" exists: the financier owns the system 5–10 years
  (to claim the commercial credit), then transfers it. Enphase and Freedom
  Forever launched versions.
- [fact] Standard lease/PPA escalators: 0.99% / 1.99% / 2.99% per year;
  terms typically 20–25 years.
- [fact] AZ's state credit remains: 25% of cost, capped at $1,000, plus
  sales-tax and property-tax exemptions.
- [fact] Pricing: Phoenix metro ~$2.30/W (EnergySage, July 2026), average
  system 14.56 kW, pre-incentive cost ~$33,511 (range ~$28,484–$38,538);
  statewide AZ average also $2.30/W, vs ~$2.58/W national.
- [fact] Export rates: APS 6.171¢/kWh (RCP, locked 10 yrs by
  interconnection vintage) · SRP flat 3.45¢ (net billing since its Nov
  2025 net-metering retirement for new customers) · TEP ~5.7¢. All far
  below 13–15¢ retail — export economics have compressed, and any pitch
  that skips this is incomplete.
- [fact] Battery attach rate nationally FELL 41% → 38%; storage ~$1,074/kWh
  average. APS Storage Rewards ~$110/kW/season (~$660); TEP ~$120/kW.
- [fact] SRP: base service charge raised ~50% in Nov 2025 (~$30–32/mo);
  legacy demand plans ~$9.16/kW on-peak, sunsetting Nov 2029; grandfathered
  terms do NOT transfer at home sale.

## The trust crisis (the credibility opening)

- [fact] Installer failures: Titan Solar Power (Mesa HQ) Chapter 7
  mid-2024 ("solar orphans") · SunPower Chapter 11 Aug 2024 · Sunnova
  Chapter 11 June 9, 2025 · Freedom Forever Chapter 11 April 15, 2026
  (~190,000 systems affected, $0.5–1B liabilities). Roughly 100 U.S. solar
  companies have gone bankrupt since 2023.
- [fact] AZ AG enforcement: Sunrun/Vivint consent agreement, $600,000
  restitution, approved May 22, 2025 · Vision Solar/Solar Xchange $13.8M
  penalty (2023, joint AG/DOJ/FTC — misrepresenting utility affiliation) ·
  Tucson Solar Pros owner indicted Aug 2024 (up to $695k fraud) · AEOS
  Energy ROC license revoked 2019 · Suntria: 125 BBB complaints in 3 years.
- [fact] Documented tactic categories: forged signatures, "free solar"
  claims, fake utility/government affiliation, post-install ghosting,
  dealer-fee-inflated loans (APR padded to hide commission), undisclosed
  UCC-1 liens, buried escalators, high-pressure door-to-door.
- [fact] AZ law: 3-day cooling-off period on door-to-door contracts.
  HOA law (§33-439) voids HOA solar bans, caps HOA-imposed cost adds at 15%.
- [survey] Distrust of solar companies DOUBLED 22% → 44% of homeowners
  (2023→2024, Aurora Solar).

## The objection landscape (ranked by survey frequency)

1. [survey] Up-front cost — 57% (SolarTech 2025), 75% (Aurora 2024).
2. [survey] Doubt about savings/ROI — 62%.
3. [survey] Distrust of solar companies — 44% and rising (see above).
4. [fact] Financing cost — median solar loan ~7.5% in H1 2025.
5. [survey] Knowledge gap — only 16% understand net metering; only 34%
   know their state incentives. (The educational opening.)
6. [survey] Secondary: maintenance 15% · aesthetics 9–31% · renter/not
   decision-maker 26% (63% of renters) · moving plans · roof ~30%.

## What content converts

- [fact, general not solar-specific] HubSpot 2026: short-form video top
  marketer ROI (49%) · content marketing ≈3x leads of outbound at 62%
  lower cost (Demand Metric) · email top B2C channel (~$40:$1).

## The consultant-not-salesperson niche

- [fact] No dominant consumer-advocacy solar creator exists at scale.
  Closest: Will Prowse (~1.1M subs, DIY/affiliate — different model) ·
  Solar Surge (~101k, industry-facing) · James the Solar Expert (~15k).
- [fact] Small fee-only models exist: True Solar Consulting $199/hr or
  $30/mo · quote-review services $250–1,000. [weak-evidence] Viability at
  scale unproven; some homeowners will pay $100–250 for review, many
  expect free.
- [fact — citable framework] The six-question advisor-vs-salesperson test
  (Solar Resource USA): (1) Is pay tied to system size? (2) Transparent
  contact info? (3) Shows competing bids? (4) Walks the contract language?
  (5) Models YOUR usage, not a national average? (6) Will they say "solar
  isn't worth it for your home"?

## Positioning takeaways (owner-aligned)

1. The rate case is real, near-term, quantifiable — give sourced numbers,
   not vague warnings.
2. AZ price history BEAT the national average — the nuanced, less-alarmist
   claim is the credibility play.
3. The credit expiration changed financing more than rates did — most
   homeowners don't know; strong educational ground.
4. Installer bankruptcies + AG cases are the trust opening — calm,
   documented walk-throughs occupy underserved territory.
5. The neutral-consultant niche is genuinely open at scale.

## Installer quality — VERIFIED, TWICE (see az-installer-quality-2026.md)

Two research passes verified and then sharpened the owner's professional
knowledge with named, dated sources. Full labeled source of truth is
`az-installer-quality-2026.md`; headlines:

- [fact] Program names + exact requirements confirmed: **APS Qualified
  Technology Installer** (2yr+ same-name experience, A+ BBB, 3yr+ design/
  install experience) and **SRP Preferred Solar Installer** (24mo+, BBB
  A+ with zero outstanding complaints, ≤2 ROC complaints in 2 years).
  Manufacturer tiers verified by name: Tesla Certified Installer ·
  Enphase Platinum/Gold/Silver (explicitly NOT Enphase's liability) ·
  SolarEdge Preferred Partner + named certifications · REC Certified
  Solar Professional (unlocks the bankruptcy-surviving ProTrust warranty)
  · Panasonic Authorized Installer (⚠ Panasonic discontinued its solar/
  battery line in 2026 — still honors existing warranties) · Qcells
  Q.PARTNER (lighter program, no homeowner tier ladder).
- [fact] Three-course flashing (cement/fabric/cement) CONFIRMED at the
  UNDERLAYMENT layer on tile roofs by manufacturer install guides
  (IronRidge) — tiles removed, standoff into structure, underlayment
  penetration three-coursed on all four sides, tiles cut to fit. Shingle
  roofs weave flashing into courses per Atlas Roofing's PV detail; foam/
  membrane roofs are a roof-system-COMPATIBILITY problem (manufacturer-
  approved primer/adhesive), not just a penetration question.
- [fact] NREL SolarAPP+ data: 58–78% of inspection failures are
  WORK-QUALITY issues, not equipment. SolarGrade field inspections found
  wiring/connector issues in 59–83% of projects. Workmanship, not brand,
  is the dominant real-world risk.
- [fact] ARS 44-1762 warranty minimums (2yr device+install / 1yr rest);
  three distinct warranty buckets (product/performance/workmanship) with
  workmanship typically only 1–10yr unless the installer voluntarily goes
  further — 10+ years is the bar to look for.
- [fact] ROC classes: CR-11/R-11 Electrical (the baseline) + R-42
  Roofing (matters more when the solar co. takes contractual
  responsibility for roof repair itself). Public license + complaint
  lookup is free; the license holder answers for subcontractors.
- [fact — UPGRADED from industry-claim] CFPB (2024, reiterated June 2026):
  dealer fees can inflate financed loan principal by **30% OR MORE**
  above cash price, often hidden inside principal rather than reflected
  in the stated APR. On Phoenix's $2.30/W average, that's roughly $6,900
  of pure financing-structure inflation on a 10kW system — far larger
  than any real quality premium (~$0.05–0.20/W, ~$500–2,000 on 10kW).
- [owner rule, standing] Price per watt = transparency tool, never the
  verdict. Content never names specific installers.
