## The test every review applies
Scroll the grid cold, as a Buckeye homeowner with a $400 bill:
1. Does this look like a real person who does this work every day?
2. Is it obviously LOCAL (Valley light, tile roofs, desert streets)?
3. Do I see proof-of-work (installs, equipment, consults) — not just graphics?
4. Would I trust them with my roof — and is there an easy, soft next step?
A feed of nothing but designed cards fails #3. A feed of nothing but
scenery fails #1. The mix is the product.

## Grid composition rules (v1 — tune with audit data)
- Ratio target: roughly 2 photo-feeling posts for every 1 designed card.
- No two designed cards adjacent in the grid; no two same-look posts
  adjacent (the app's pack rotation helps, the Feed Director enforces).
- Pinned trio = positioning: (1) who this page is for, (2) the flagship
  education piece, (3) proof-of-work.
- Every 6–9 posts, one piece of visible proof-of-work (real install/
  equipment photo).
- Reels without going on camera: screen-recorded bill/rate-plan
  walkthroughs, real install b-roll from the photo dump, voiceover over
  stills. Face optional, presence mandatory.

## Authenticity rules (binding — this protects the whole brand)
The page's entire moat is trust in a market where distrust doubled to 44%.
One "that photo is AI" callout on a fake install destroys it. Therefore:
- **Real photos are the ONLY proof-of-work.** Anything presented as "this
  install / this meeting / this job we did" must be an actual photo from
  `brain/Brand/refs/` or Taylor's camera roll.
- **AI images are illustration, never evidence.** They depict generic,
  representative scenes (a consult at a door, a bill on a table) and are
  NEVER captioned with a specific job, date, address, customer, or "we/I
  just installed…" framing.
- Recreations of real events stay generic — no details that claim
  documentation. When in doubt, the caption voice is educational
  ("what a clean install looks like") not testimonial ("today's install").
- Respect platform AI-labeling; never attempt to strip or evade it.
- Equipment close-ups claiming craft standards (flashing, racking) must be
  REAL photos — an AI image of "proper flashing" used as proof is exactly
  the fake-authenticity the recruiting pages sell.

## Photo dump intake (what Taylor collects)
10–25 photos into `brain/Brand/refs/`, phone quality is fine — ideal set:
flashed penetrations / racking close-ups · panels on tile + shingle ·
crew or consult moments (faces ok, get consent) · service/monitoring
moments · truck/tools · Valley streets and rooflines. Each gets one line
in [[Visual Style]]: what it shows + whether it may be posted or is
style-reference-only. Uses: (a) direct posts — the proof-of-work lane,
(b) Higgsfield style reference so AI illustration matches the REAL
photos' light and texture (Soul moodboard/style_id once enough exist).
