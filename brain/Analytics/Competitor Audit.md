# Competitor & Content-Model Audit

How we study successful service-based professionals (realtors above all —
they cracked local trust content years ago) and measure our queue against
them. Findings feed [[Editorial Direction]] and [[Visual Style]].

## Why realtors, not solar reps

Owner observation (confirmed by the July 2026 research): solar reps on IG
mostly run RECRUITING content — commission receipts, sold-deal shoutouts,
"join my team" sales-leader positioning. Almost nobody markets as a local
homeowner consultant. That's the open lane. So the models to study are:
1. **Local realtors with educational followings** (the primary model)
2. **Other trusted service pros** (inspectors, lenders, insurance
   educators) who make expertise watchable
3. **Solar recruiting pages** — studied only as the ANTI-PATTERN library

## Grid-level audit (owner priority, 2026-07-20 — this IS the review)

Taylor's ask is explicitly NOT an analytics review — it's "make my page
LOOK AND FEEL like an established local pro," judged the way a homeowner
actually experiences a profile: they land on it and scroll the grid for
2 seconds before deciding if this is a real, active professional. The
[[Feed Director]] owns running this test and applying findings to grid
composition. Score each high-follower account on:

- **Grid rhythm**: what % of the 9-30 visible tiles are photo-feeling
  (proof-of-work, candid) vs. designed cards vs. text-only? What's the
  repeat pattern (e.g. photo-photo-card, photo-card-photo)?
- **Proof-of-work visibility**: how often does a real job/meeting/result
  appear, and how is it framed (candid vs. staged, face-forward vs.
  hands/detail shots)?
- **Profile-level signals**: pinned posts (what do they lead with?),
  Highlights categories, bio structure, Reels cover consistency.
- **"Does this look active TODAY"**: recency cues, Stories rings,
  cadence — what makes a page feel currently-run vs. abandoned.

This feeds directly into [[Feed Director]]'s grid plan — not just into
Adopt/Avoid bullet points.

## The scoring rubric (per-post detail, secondary to the grid-level view)

**Per account** (fill one row per account below):
handle · niche · metro · followers · avg likes + comments over the last 12
posts · engagement rate (likes+comments ÷ followers) · posting cadence ·
format mix (reel/carousel/single %) · face presence % · overall vibe in
5 words

**Per standout post** (their top 2–3 each):
- Hook: the exact first-frame / first-line words
- **On-screen word count** (the key metric — how little text is on screen?)
- Where the education lives: screen / caption / comments
- Format + length · production style (phone-authentic ↔ designed)
- CTA type (none / soft / hard) and exact wording
- Why it worked, in one sentence

**Compare to our queue**: for each queued post, score the same fields and
note the gap (e.g. "our slide 2 = 9 on-screen words ✓; theirs average 7").

## Adopt / Avoid (living lists)

**Adopt** *(seeded from owner direction, 2026-07-20)*:
- Screen text = summary (≤ ~12 words/slide); education in caption/comments
- Realistic imagery over designed templates for trust content
- Soft CTAs, always

**Avoid** *(the solar anti-pattern)*:
- Commission receipts, sold-deal shoutouts, recruiting language
- Wall-of-text slides that look like a lecture
- Hard urgency CTAs
